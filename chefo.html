CHALLENGE

Both foodies and non cooks can agree that deciding what to eat every day is tough, when you’re short on time or don’t have the ingredients needed. Current apps don’t consider personal preferences or lifestyles, and toggling between a googled recipe and the kitchen is inconvienient and frustrating.




SOLUTION

Chef O helps you decide what to make with the ingredients you already have, making home cooking easy without feeling overwhelmed. Recipes are fast, fresh and easy with personal health filters to help users focus on a healthy lifestyle.



DISCOVERY

Working with UX mentors, I led the research, UX and visual process of this project. I conducted several user surveys to understand potential users and their cooking frustrations. 85% stated their biggest frustration when cooking, was not having the ingredients needed, followed by not knowing what to make. I also reseached various cooking apps such as Yummly and AllRecipies.com to see what users liked and disliked about current platforms. Based on user research and competitor analysis, I created user personas that helped guide my design process.



Personas

Karen is the ultimate foodie but doesn’t always have time to cook at home for her family of 3. As a Program Manager, her days are full of team meetings and deadlines leaving her little time and energy to cook. She’s frustrated with not knowing what to cook when she gets home, or missing ingredients she needs for a recipe she googled. As a user, Karen wants fresh, simple recipes she can make anytime with every day ingredients.






User Flows

Based on users like Karen and other frustrated busy cooks, I discovered there is still a need for an easy, clean, and engaging cooking experience. I created user flows focused on finding recipes based on categories and ingredients, to help the user make a quick cooking decision.






IDEATION

Using the user flows and personas I created, I sketched wireframes to refine the UI and visual elements. Using IOS guidelines and current design trends, I transitioned my sketches to high-fi wireframes to maintain a consistent, clean flow to keep users focused.





simultaneously, I created the brand concept to align with the user goals and app flow. I was ultimately inspired by my bearded foodie husband, Omneet. As a non cook myself, I was fortunate to marry an amazing cook who can come up with great meals using simple, every day ingredient; a major user goal. Taking this idea with stocklogos, Chef O was created, an energized, reliable chef who can tell you what to eat and how to make it.



User Testing

Throughout the wireframe process, I tested various UX and visual elements to ensure an engaging experience. For example, a major user goal is to browse and add ingredients you already have, to find recipies. Below are three versions of this experience, which was tested using preference and click tests. Users found Option B, the most easy to use and get started.



Testing visual design was also important to highlight easy cooking and a memorable brand. Users found version B engaging but also intimidating. Ultimately, version A was best as it helped the user focus on the filter options and the end goal.



CONCLUSION

The final part of my design process was using InVision to build the interactive prototype, which can be viewed below. This project taught me how to overcome UI challenges, such as how to categorize items and working with conflicting user feedback. My goal was to create an inspiring recipe app for easy, everyday cooking which Chef O allows you to do.
