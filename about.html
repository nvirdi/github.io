Hi, I’m Navnit, a UX designer based in San Francisco with passion for solving user problems based on research, strategy and creativity.







DESIGN AT FIRST SIGHT

Although design wasn’t initially on my journey, I naturally found myself in design oriented roles without even knowing it. Growing up in Seattle, I studied business administration and did various marketing roles for start ups and mid size companies, yet I wasn't feeling challenged or fulfilled.

One of my first jobs was a media coordinator for a creative agency, where I connected car brands with online auto influencers. Even with all the auto connections, our program was not growing and content not engagning. Seeing this problem, I thought, why not reach out to non auto influencers and redesign our website to include this new content. I started reaching out to popular beauty bloggers (I know, how do you connect makeup with cars?), who surprisingly were open to working with auto brands and presented viral content. From there, I worked with the creative team to redesign the website and expand the program. For the first time I felt accomplished and started to think about a career in design or technology, but I wasn’t sure if this path was for me.




USING DESIGN TO HELP PEOPLE

Fast forward to my most recent role at Sutter Health, I found myself again, drawn to solving problems using design. As a project coordinator, I performed data analysis for hospital programs and helped speciality departments with administrative logistics. At the same time, department directors were reaching out to me for help with various design projects: new office layouts, patient surveys, program pamphlets, project templates and more.

My last project with Sutter was working with our engineering team on building a new, mobile navigation app. Here, I redesigned the hospital map so patients can get around our complicated hospital better. My role went from tedious Excel tasks to helping solve a patient experience problem using design. Helping patients make their lives easier, made my job more rewarding. As I was telling my husband this over dinner one night, he said to me “Have you ever thought about UX Design?” and that’s exactly what I did. Feeling inspired, I started to rethink my career again and this time I was sure about design.




TAKING THE LEAP

I submerged myself into a design apprenticeship program where I not only created responsive and engaging applications, but was mentored by experienced senior designers. I learned the tools and practices, but most importantly how to be a confident designer and ok with failures. For example, when building the map screen for a hotel search app, users initially found the map overwhelming. At first, I was frustrated trying to figure out what I can change and I didn’t feel good enough to be a designer. I learned how to channel this energy into motivation and look at my “failures” as opportunities instead. I was able to recreate a more user friendly map view through in depth user interviews and more design research.

From user surveys to building wireframes, to creating intuitive prototypes based on research, I continuously improve my skills and dedicate myself to being a well rounded designer. So as I mentioned before, design wasn’t initially on my journey, but I've always been designing. I aim to work for an organization that lets me design solutions for user problems in a collaborative and growing environment.
