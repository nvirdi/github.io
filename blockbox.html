BLOCBOX

Research | Branding | Wireframes | Prototype


Blocbox is a web application that lets you collect and store information in one space. You can create simple documents, store inspiring images, and add links while collaborating with others.


VIEW PROTOTYPE







PROBLEM

When it comes to saving content there are many cloud based platforms to use, however they can have confusing dashboards or lack certain sharing capabilities.

SOLUTION

Blocbox lets users collect images, notes and links while colloborating with others. Users can experience a more simple and intuitive way to organize their information.













RESEARCH

After reviewing the Blocbox business and design requirements, I surveyed over 50 potential users to understand how they currently save and share content. Below are the main findings from the survey results:



90% Browse information for work and news
90% Take notes for personal and business items
70% Download or bookmark information
80% Work in a team based environment
70% Found limited sharing with current apps
60% Use social media to sign up for accounts

    WIREFRAMES

    Simultaneously, I created wireframes while developing the visual design. Below is an example of my wireframe process with the Blocbox landing page. Within the landing page, it was important to highlight what Blocbox is and the features it offers. Users found the initial landing page layout confusing as the features were spread out. Taking this feedback, I modified this section to list the features in a row for better scanning.








    Another wireframe example is the Blocbox account settings page. Users found the page too busy with input fields, making upgrades a lengthy process. Again, taking this feedback, the account settings page was modified to include a payment modal box, showing less input fields on the page and reducing confusion.

    USER TESTING

    Users were asked to upgrade their account from Free to Professional and add a piece of content (image, link or note). When upgrading accounts, 75% of users stated there was no indication their profile was updated after adding payment. This lead to highlighting their current account status initially so they know which account plan they have and can easily upgrade.








    When asked to add content to their dashboard, most users suggested a search bar to easily find their items. To learn more, I performed a preference test on different positions of the Add button and search bar. 80% of users chose to have the search bar on the right and the add button closer to the left side menu. Using this feedback I was able to apply improvements to the design and create a better user experience.
