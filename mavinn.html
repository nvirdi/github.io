MAVINN

Research | Branding | Wireframes | Prototype


Mavinn is a mobile app where you can find nearby hotels catered to your travel needs. Using unique filters, you can search hotels best suited for your occasion.


VIEW PROTOTYPE







PROBLEM

As last minute travel continue to be a common hobby, current hotel apps don't always hit the mark when it comes to finding a unique hotel that the user enjoys.

SOLUTION

With Mavinn, you can filter hotels based on your occasion making the staycation process stress free. Reserve unique in a fast and easy manner.













RESEARCH

To get to know the user, I surveyed over 50 respondants on how they currently book hotels and why. Below are the main survey findings that lead me to developing Mavinn:



80% Reserve hotels for vacations or work
75% Preferred hotel experience over location
90% Reviewed hotel ratings before booking
60% Found current hotel apps complicated
70% Reserve hotels often or last minute
66% reserve hotels for special occasions

    BRANDING

    When developing the brand, users wanted an organized, fast and friendly application which brought me to the name Mavinn, a combination of “Maven” and “Inn” or in other words an expert in hotels. The name is simple, playful, and reflects reliability which is what Mavinn is all about. When sketching logo concepts, I started with images of beds to help reflect comfort. Although hotel beds show comfort, I found this imagery took away from the exciting part of finding a unique hotel, which is what users wanted. This lead to the final logo concept of colorful buildings, a reflection of unique hotels.

    USER TESTING

    To further refine the design, I asked users to reserve Hotel Zetta and let me know their likes and dislikes in terms of ease and experience. Below are key findings that helped shape the final app design:






    60% of users found the map screen overwhelming at first glance, which led to unique map icons for each filter. This helps the user browse hotels faster. 80% of users clicked on the hotel card vs the initial "view" button. Taking this feedback, I made the entire hotel card clickable and removed the unneccesary button









    Taking this feedback, I applied changes to the wireframes and final design. These changes included an onboarding process to help guide new users, a map filter initially selected for guidance, and changing the reservation screen to display information more clearly for easy readability and editing. I learned quickly how to implement changes throughout the design process to ensure a user-friendly design.









    PROTOTYPE

    The Mavinn prototype was given to users to interact with who overall enjoyed the map simplicity. Although there are many hotel apps already out there, Mavinn helps solve a unique problem for travelers. I learned that understanding the user's problem in detail really helps define the design direction. To further develop this product, I would continue user testing and complete an IOS version.
