Mavinn


Mavinn is a mobile application where users can find nearby hotels catered to their travel or occasion. With special filters, users can find unique hotels.




PROBLEM

With so many hotel apps, finding a hotel is easy. However, finding a hotel that fits your occasion is not and requires much more research and frustration.

SOLUTION

Whether it’s a weekend getaway or business trip, finding a hotel that’s appropriate for the occasion is important. Mavinn helps find those unique hotels.



Research

To better understand the user, I surveyed over 50 respondants on how they currently book hotels and why. Below are the findings from the survey:

80% Reserve hotels for vacations or work
75% Preferred hotel experience over location
90% Reviewed hotel ratings before booking
60% Found current hotel apps complicated
70% Reserve hotel rooms often or last minute
66% reserve hotels for special occasions

Based on these survey results, users were more frustrated with finding specific or special hotel types. To further understand this issue, I interviewed three potential users on their current hotel booking process:

After getting to know the users, a competitive analysis was completed to learn more about current solutions. Below are more well known hotel applications that allow users to search, reserve, and review hotels.

Hotels Tonight lets users reserve available rooms up to 7 days in advance or day of.

Users Liked:

  • Simple and flows well
  • Catered to city enthusiasts

Users Disliked:

  • Helpful for certain cities
  • Map View can be overwhelming at first glance

Hotels.com allows users to search nearby hotels, book, and review

Users Liked:

  • Easy to navigate through
  • Can save hotels for later reference

Users Disliked:

  • Lists hotels by location only
  • Annoying to toggle between map and list view

Booking.com allows users to search hotel and vacation rentals, reserve and review.

Users Liked:

  • Detailed reviews
  • Can search any property or hotel

Users Disliked:

  • Room selection layout is confusing
  • Map view and list view are seperate

Branding

Emphasizing reliability and simplicity was key when creating the brand identity for Mavinn. Using a word association tool, I gathered words that helped convey a sense of comfort and catered to the different types of user experiences.

Users wanted an organized, fast and friendly application which brought me to the name Mavinn, combination of “Maven” and “Inn” or in other words an expert in hotels. The name is simple, playful, and reflects reliability.

When sketching logo concepts, I immediately started with images of beds to help reflect comfort. Although hotel beds are common and do show comfort, I found this imagery took away from the exciting part of finding a unique hotel, which is what users wanted. After conducting preference tests, users were more excited about logo option 2 with the bold color palette.

To further develop the visual design, colors and type were chosen to emphasize the Mavinn brand concept. Using bright but complimentary colors with a simple, straight forward font like Roboto, helped create a balanced and clean visual. The user can easily focus on searching hotels and get excited to reserve them for their special occasion.

User Testing

To further refine the design, I asked users to do the following navigation tests: Reserve Hotel Zetta, Write a Review and Add a Payment from their account settings. I also conducted preference tests to understand what users liked or disliked, below are the findings from these various user tests:


Map & Reservation Flow Feedback:

  • Users found details view overwhelming - too much going on
  • Map screen filters are confusing, not sure what they do at first
  • Users preferred to click on the hotel card in list view, not just the name
  • Users liked all reservation info on one screen as opposed to different steps

Write Review & Add Payment Feedback:

  • Need more guidance when initially starting application
  • Users liked the add payment option in more than one place
  • Users preferred to leave a review by going back to reservations

Taking this feedback, I applied changes to the wireframes and final design. These changes included an onboarding process for new users,, having a map filter initially selected for guidance, and seperating the amenities and reviews so users feel comfortable reserving.



Final Design

The Mavinn prototype was given to users to interact with who overall enjoyed the map simplicity and the idea of hotel filters. Although there are many hotel apps already out there, Mavinn helps solve a unique problem for a special group of travelers who value where they are staying. As travel becomes a common hobby, different places to stay become more important to the user which is shown in platforms like Air BnB and other rental applications.
