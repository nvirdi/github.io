MAVINN

Research | Branding | Wireframes | Prototype


Mavinn is a mobile application where you find nearby hotels catered to your travel needs. Using unique filters, you can search hotels best suited for your occasion.


VIEW PROTOTYPE







PROBLEM

When searching for a hotel, there are many apps to use however few show hotels that are specific to a travelers needs. As last minute travel and staycations continue to be a common hobby, finding unique hotels become more difficult.

SOLUTION

Whether it’s a family getaway, anniversary trip, or business conference, finding a hotel that’s appropriate for your occasion should be stress free. With Mavinn, you can filter hotels based on your event so you can stay happily at your hotel.













RESEARCH

To get to know the user, I surveyed over 50 respondants on how they currently book hotels and why. Below are the main survey findings:



80% Reserve hotels for vacations or work
75% Preferred hotel experience over location
90% Reviewed hotel ratings before booking
60% Found current hotel apps complicated
70% Reserve hotels often or last minute
66% reserve hotels for special occasions

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

After getting to know the users and their frustrations, a competitive analysis was completed to learn more about current solutions. Below are well known hotel applications that lets users search, reserve, and review hotels.


Hotels Tonight lets users reserve available rooms up to 7 days in advance or day of.

Users Liked

  • Simple, sleek and flows well
  • Catered to city enthusiasts

Users Disliked

  • Helpful for certain cities only
  • Map view is overwhelming at first glance

Hotels.com allows users to search nearby hotels, book, and review.

Users Liked:

  • Easy to navigate through
  • Can save hotels for later

Users Disliked:

  • Lists hotels by location only
  • Annoying to toggle between map and list view

Booking.com allows users to search hotel and vacation rentals, reserve and review.

Users Liked:

  • Detailed reviews
  • Can search any hotel

Users Disliked:

  • Room selection is confusing
  • Map view and list view are seperate

BRANDING

When developing the brand, users wanted an organized, fast and friendly application which brought me to the name Mavinn, a combination of “Maven” and “Inn” or in other words an expert in hotels. The name is simple, playful, and reflects reliability which is what Mavinn is all about. When sketching logo concepts, I immediately started with images of beds to help reflect comfort. Although hotel beds are common and do show comfort, I found this imagery took away from the exciting part of finding a unique hotel, which is what users wanted. This lead to the final logo concept of colorful buildings, a reflection of unique hotels.

TESTING

To refine the design, I asked users to do the following navigation tests: Reserve Hotel Zetta, Write a Review and Add a Payment from their account settings. I also conducted preference tests to understand what users liked or disliked, below is the user feedback:



Map & Reservation Flow

  • Users found details view overwhelming
  • Users liked reservation info on one screen only
  • Users preferred to click on the hotel card not just the name

Write Review & Add Payment

  • Need more guidance when starting application
  • Users liked the add payment option in two places
  • Users preferred to leave reviews by going back to reservations





Taking user feedback, I applied changes to the wireframes and created the final design. These changes included an onboarding process for new users so they know how to use the app, having a map filter initially selected for guidance, and seperating the amenities and reviews so users feel comfortable reserving. I learned throughout this process how to quickly implement changing ideas into my design to create a more user friendly app.





PROTOTYPE

The final Mavinn prototype was given to users to interact with who overall enjoyed the map simplicity. Although there are many hotel apps already out there, Mavinn helps solve a unique problem for travelers. I learned that understanding the user's problem in detail really helps define the design direction.
