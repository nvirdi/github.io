CHALLENGE

As the beauty community continues to grow with new brands, trends and products, makeup users will agree many don’t cater to all skintones. Women of deeper skintones find it frustrating to shop for makeup when they can’t find a shade that matches their unique skintone.




SOLUTION

The Playground offers more diverse shades with skintone details included. This helps all makeup users easily find lipstick shades that compliment their skin, while creating a more inclusive and empowering beauty community.



DISCOVERY

As a former makeup artist and lover of all things beauty, finding products that perfectly match my skin has always been a struggle. Until recently, many brands did not include products that catered to all skintones, creating a social divide between brands and users. I surveyed over 50 potential users to understand their makeup shopping frustrations, 75% stated products don’t match their skintone. 80% choose lipsticks as their favorite products to shop, preferring formula over brand and price.

Taking this research, I also analyzed popular lipstick brands such as Bite Beauty and Giella, each offering custom lipstick shades. 100% of users agreed each platform did not offer enough shades to choose from or they did not know how to find the right shade.



Personas

Nicole is a makeup enthusiast with a go getter lifestyle. With her odd shift hours, she doesn’t always have time to shop in store before events and wants more go to products she can purchase regularly. Her biggest frustration is trying new brands/products and being disappointed with the color pay off. She relies heavily on reviews and has a hard time finding quality reviews from people that look similar to her. As a user, Nicole wants unique lipstick shades that have high quality pigment and compliment her skin.






User Flows

Users like Nicole share a common goal: quickly browse shades that actually match their skin. I created a three step user flow based on common lipstick qualities: Select Shade, Select Finish, and Select Package. This helps the user focus on the custom lipstick process without feeling overwhelmed






IDEATION

When creating the Playground identity, it was important to incorporate lipstick formula and the shopping habits of users. I created wireframes that lead users through a customization process to help them find the best shade. When brainstorming, I decided to use my own liptstick products to help inspire potential logos. The term “swatch” and “play” stood out as they conveyed a fun and common way to explore makeup and helped guide my design process.







User Testing

I created user tests to ensure visual elements and flows make sense to the user. With a focus on customization, 73% of users voted for Option B, using images to choose their lipstick finish as oppose to buttons. Having a visual idea of what the lipstick finish will look like helps the user create a more personalized lipstick








CONCLUSION

Using InVision, I built the Playground prototype which can be viewed below. Overall users enjoyed their experience, yet I would like to continue revising the visual design for an optimal makeup experience. The Playground helps solve a real life problem for women of different skintones, including myself who want to feel confident with their makeup decisions. My goal was to show makeup as more attainable and inclusive which I hope to see with future products.
